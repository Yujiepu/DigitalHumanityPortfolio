About Me


I was born in central China, received my Master's degree in History at Chinese University of Hong Kong and pursued my Doctoral degree in East Asian Languages & Cultures at University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign.I specialize in Chinese history, specifically in the intersection of law, medicine, and madness in late imperial China. My dissertation, Legal Justice, Medical Authority and Madness in Qing China (1644-1912), examines the intersection of medical and legal practices in the construction and regulation of madness during the Qing dynasty.
