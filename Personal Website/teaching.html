In teaching East Asian history, I weave a narrative connecting vast socio-political landscapes with intimate personal stories. My Modern Chinese History course delves into individual tales like that of a Chinese sailor in 1881, sending cookies from Britain amidst the tensions of the impending Sino-Japanese War. Meanwhile, the course Mental Health in East Asia, Past and Present explores the interplay of societal pressures and personal tragedies, using sources such as The Memoirs of Lady Hyegyeong, which details the Crown Prince Sado incident in Choson Korea. This narrative-driven method underscores my philosophy: understanding history requires navigating both its grand epochs and individual stories, fostering both critical thinking and empathy in my students.
