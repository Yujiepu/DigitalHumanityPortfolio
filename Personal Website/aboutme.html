About Me


I am Yujie (Elena) Pu, Lecturer/Instructor of Imperial Chinese History in the Department of East Asian Languages and Cultures at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). I earned my Ph.D. from UIUC in 2025 and hold a Master of Arts in History from the Chinese University of Hong Kong.

My research explores the intersection of crime, law, and medicine in late imperial China, with a focus on how judicial and medical authorities constructed, institutionalized, and regulated madness under the Qing dynasty. I am currently developing a book manuscript titled Legal Justice, Medical Authority, and Madness in China, 1600-1930, and conducting comparative studies on Euro-Asian histories of insanity, doctor-patient relationships, Manchu medical texts, and the pharmaceutical industry in East Asia.

At UIUC, I teach both broad survey courses, such as Contemporary East Asia, Chinese Civilization, China Under the Qing Dynasty, and History of Medicine in China. My work has been supported by a 2025 National Science Foundation Travel Grant from History of Science Society and a fellowship of Inclusive Pedagogy Certificate Program at UIUC's Office of Diversity, Equity & Inclusion.

As a first-generation college student who has studied across Asia and the United States, I bring diverse educational perspectives into every classroom. By sharing real-life narratives of navigating Eastern and Western academic terrains, I aim to foster an inclusive environment where each student feels empowered to contribute their unique story to our collective discourse.
